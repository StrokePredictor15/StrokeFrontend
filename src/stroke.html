Understanding Stroke

Your guide to recognizing, preventing, and responding to strokes.

What is a Stroke?

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of your brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes, making strokes a medical emergency.

Stroke Statistics

  • Globally, 1 in 4 adults over 25 will suffer a stroke in their lifetime.
  • Stroke is the 2nd leading cause of death worldwide.
  • Over 12 million people experience a stroke each year.
  • 5.5 million deaths are caused by stroke annually.

Common Causes of Stroke

  • High blood pressure (Hypertension)
  • Heart disease
  • Diabetes
  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • High cholesterol
  • Obesity and sedentary lifestyle

Why is Stroke Serious?

Stroke can cause permanent brain damage, long-term disability, or death. Acting quickly saves lives. Recognizing the early signs and seeking immediate treatment can significantly improve outcomes.

Remember FAST

  • Face drooping
  • Arm weakness
  • Speech difficulty
  • Time to call emergency services

Stay aware. Stay prepared. Save lives.
